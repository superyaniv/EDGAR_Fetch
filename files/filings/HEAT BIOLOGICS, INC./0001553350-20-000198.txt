<BODY style="margin-top:0;font-family:Times New Roman; font-size:10pt; color:#000000">
<P style="margin:0px; font-family:Calibri,Times New Roman; font-size:12pt">&nbsp;</P>
<P style="margin:0px"><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0px; font-size:11pt" align=right><B>EXHIBIT 99.1</B></P>
<P style="margin:0px"><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0px; font-size:11pt" align=center><B>Heat Biologics Announces Research Collaboration with University of Miami to Develop Vaccine Designed to Protect Against COVID-19 Coronavirus </B></P>
<P style="margin:0px" align=center><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0px; font-size:11pt" align=center><I>Heat&#146;s gp96 vaccine platform activates CD8 T cells, antigen presenting cells and natural killer cells, and induces mucosal immunity which could make it an ideal vaccine for COVID-19</I></P>
<P style="margin:0px"><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0px; font-size:11pt" align=center><I>Vaccine designed to induce a multi-epitope specific CD8 T-cell response, which is crucial for protection against potential future mutations of COVID-19 </I></P>
<P style="margin:0px"><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0px; font-size:11pt">DURHAM, N.C., March 5, 2020 (ACCESSWIRE) -- Heat Biologics, Inc. (Nasdaq:HTBX), a clinical-stage biopharmaceutical company specialized in the development of therapeutic vaccines, announced today a strategic collaboration with the University of Miami Miller School of Medicine to support the development of a vaccine leveraging Heat&#146;s proprietary gp96 platform designed to target the <I>SARS-CoV-2 </I>coronavirus that causes COVID-19. </P>
<P style="margin:0px"><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0px; font-size:11pt">Clinical and preclinical studies suggest that Heat&#146;s gp96-based vaccines may be utilized to target COVID-19. Heat has treated more than 300 patients to date with its gp96-based therapeutic vaccines. &nbsp;Results from these studies together with positive outcomes in NIH and DOD-funded mouse and primate studies against SIV/HIV 1-2, malaria and zika, demonstrate that gp96 vaccines express a broad range of antigens and stimulate a robust systemic immune response, culminating in humoral and cell mediated responses in different organs including the gut, reproductive tract, liver and lungs. &nbsp;</P>
<P style="margin:0px"><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0px; font-size:11pt">Heat&#146;s COVID-19 vaccine will utilize Heat&#146;s gp96 platform to generate open docking sites for the insertion of multiple <I>SARS-CoV-2</I> antigens. Heat anticipates that its novel approach should activate a potent immune response, without the disadvantages of possible genomic integration of foreign DNA or viral vector instability possible with attenuated viral vaccines. This approach is designed to induce a multi-epitope specific memory CD8 T-cell response that protects against multiple, distinct coronavirus strains across diverse human populations and against potential future mutations of SARS-CoV-2 and other coronavirus. </P>
<P style="margin:0px"><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0px; font-size:11pt">Heat&#146;s COVID-19 program emerged from the same laboratory that originally developed Heat&#146;s gp96 platform technology, and will be developed at the University of Miami Miller School of Medicine under the direction of Natasa Strbo, M.D., D.Sc., research assistant professor of microbiology and immunology, who has spent many years advancing the gp96 platform as a vaccine against HIV, malaria, zika and other infectious diseases. </P>
<P style="margin:0px"><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0px; font-size:11pt">Mucosal immunity and airway memory T-cell responses are crucial in the protection against respiratory viruses such as <I>SARS-CoV-2</I>, since these areas are the first to encounter the virus. Importantly, Heat&#146;s gp96-based vaccines have demonstrated effectiveness in the induction of mucosal immunity. Dr. Strbo and her team have developed a gp96 vaccine against SIV (the primate equivalent of HIV) that has been shown to induce a dramatic antigen-specific immune response in the mucous membranes. Treated primates (rheusus macaques) were 73 percent less likely to acquire a particularly virulent form of the SIV virus*. These data support broader use of the vaccine platform against other viruses attacking the mucosal tissues, such as COVID-19 (<U>https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC3594107</U>). </P>
<P style="margin:0px"><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0px"><BR>
<BR></P>
<HR style="margin-bottom:9.6px; padding-top:9.6px" noshade size=1.333>
<P style="margin:0px; font-family:Calibri,Times New Roman; font-size:12pt; page-break-before:always">&nbsp;</P>
<P style="margin:0px"><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0px; font-size:11pt">&#147;T-cell immune responses play a crucial role in protection against coronaviruses and facilitation of coronavirus clearance. <FONT style="font-size:12pt">&nbsp;</FONT>It is noteworthy to mention that not antibodies, but only SARS-CoV-specific memory T-cells persisted up to 11 years post-recovery,&#148; said Dr. Strbo. &#147;Therefore, an effective coronavirus vaccine should induce neutralizing antibodies and elicit specific memory CD8 T cell responses. Currently, gp96 is the most efficient agent known to mediate antigen cross-presentation at femto-molar concentrations of antigen. Heat&#146;s gp96 vaccine platform has unique properties that enable it to activate antigen presenting cells, natural killer cells and T cells, which could make it an ideal vaccine for <I>SARS-CoV-2</I>.&#148; &nbsp;</P>
<P style="margin:0px"><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0px; font-size:11pt">Heat&#146;s wholly-owned subsidiary, Zolovax, Inc., will focus on the development of the COVID-19 vaccine. <P style="margin:0px; font-size:11pt"><B>About Heat&#146;s gp96-based therapeutic platform</B></P>
<P style="margin:0px; font-size:11pt">Heat shock protein gp96 is a chaperone protein found in all human cells. It is a potent immune adjuvant that has the role of &#147;molecular warning system&#148; or more specifically, gp96 has demonstrated a clinical ability to induce immunity against antigens from the cell it came from. gp96 is naturally tethered to the cell and is normally released only during necrosis or cell death.</P>
<P style="margin:0px"><BR></P>
<P style="margin:0px; font-size:11pt">Heat&#146;s&nbsp;proprietary gp96 vaccine platform reprograms live cells to continually secrete antigens of interest bound to the gp96, thus activating a robust T-cell response against those antigens. &nbsp;Heat&#146;s gp96 platform was developed by Dr. Eckhard Podack at the University of Miami and has undergone rigorous testing in numerous NIH and DOD-funded mice and primate trials as a vaccine against HIV, malaria and other infectious diseases, and has been tested on over 300 patients in numerous NIH and Heat-funded oncology trials.</P>
